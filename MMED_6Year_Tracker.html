Regulations

Preamble

The Masters of Medicine (Rural Health) program is designed for Papua New Guinea. Its purposes are to provide a high level of curative and preventative health services for the majority of the PNG population who continue to live in the rural and remote areas of the country, and to provide a recognized career structure and professional status for medical officers wishing to serve in rural communities.

Core Structure

  • Entry: 3rd year post-grad. Requires 2 years pre-registration + Full registration.
  • Duration: Minimum 6 years (must complete within 8).
  • Sponsorship: Must be employed by a District Hospital.
  • Exemptions: Diplomas in Child Health (DCH), O&G (DGO), or Anaesthesia (DA) grant exemption in that field.

Overview

Two streams: Part 1 Surgery (9 months total) or Part 1 O&G (3 months abbreviated).

Full Program Objectives: Interpret imaging, perform bellwether procedures independently, manage trauma and acute abdomen in rural setting.
