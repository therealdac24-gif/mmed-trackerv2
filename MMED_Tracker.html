Introduction

Rural Generalist Medicine is an academic and clinical discipline focused on primary health care. In Papua New Guinea, this extends to high-level curative and preventative care at district hospitals, often requiring significant management and leadership.

The Master of Medicine (Rural Health) is a vocational training program creating leaders responsible for District Hospital level challenges.

Regulations

Entry Requirements

  • Enterable in the 3rd year post-graduation.
  • Requires 2 years pre-registration training + Full registration.
  • Duration: Minimum 6 years (must complete within 8).

Core Structure

  • Sponsorship: Must be employed by a District Hospital. Agreement signed by Employer, Candidate, and Training Co-ordinator.
  • Part 1 Exam: Common Core + Surgery OR O&G Specialty Core. Usually by end of Year 3.
  • Attempts: 2 attempts for Part 1. 3rd requires special permission.

Minimum Supervision

  • Surgery: 3 months with accredited surgeon.
  • O&G: 3 months with accredited obstetrician.
  • Anaesthesiology: 4 months.
  • Paediatrics: 1 month.
  • Internal Medicine: 2 months.

Competencies

The 7 General Roles

  1. Professional: Ethical, honest care.
  2. Communicator: Effective listening and information sharing.
  3. Collaborator: Teamwork within health services.
  4. Medical Expert: Diagnostic and therapeutic skills.
  5. Manager: Resource allocation and leadership.
  6. Health Advocate: Identifying determinants of health.
  7. Scholar: Research, teaching, and critical appraisal.

Specific Clinical Skills (Logbook)

  • Surgery: Burns, fractures, acute abdomen, trauma.
  • Medicine: Diabetes, TB, Malaria, Snake bite, HIV.
  • Paediatrics: Neonatal resuscitation, malnutrition, severe pneumonia.
  • O&G: APH, PPH, Breech delivery, C-section.
  • Anaesthesia: CPR, Airway, Ketamine anaesthesia.

Examinations

Part 1 Exam

Usually taken after 3 years. Includes Common Core and Surgical (or O&G) Specialty Core.

Part 2 Exam (Exit)

Prerequisites: Research project submitted, Logbooks complete.

Components:

  • Written Papers (SAQ, VAQ, Public Health).
  • Clinical Exams (Short/Long cases in Surgery, Medicine, Paeds, O&G, Anaesthesia).
  • Viva Voce.
